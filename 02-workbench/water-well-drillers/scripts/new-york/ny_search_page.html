Definitions Overview
ServiceDefinitions
Drilling: Residential WellsWells constructed as a source of potable water for individual households
Drilling: Public WellsWells constructed for use as regulated public water supplies.
Drilling: Commercial WellsWells constructed for use in commercial business activities.
Drilling: Geothermal WellsWells constructed for use in space heating or cooling, or other applications involving the use or transfer of groundwater heat. For wells greater than 500 feet, refer to DEC permitting requirements.
Drilling: Agricultural WellsWells constructed for irrigation of farmland or as a water supply for livestock.
Drilling: Environmental MonitoringWells constructed to monitor the physical or chemical character of groundwater.
Drilling: Geotechnical WellsWells constructed to characterize subsurface geology or for dewatering during construction activity.
Drilling: Injection WellsWells constructed for the use of placing fluid underground. Permits for injection wells are obtained from the US EPA Underground Injection Control program.
Drilling: Irrigation WellsWells constructed for irrigation of properties such as homes and golf courses.
Drilling: Dewatering WellsWells or boreholes constructed or used for the sole purpose of lowering or controlling the level of groundwater.
Well DeepeningDrilling an existing well deeper.
Well DecommissioningPermanent closure of a well by physically removing the well from service.
Control Overflowing WellsControl of a water well such that water no longer flows beyond the well casing.
Pump InstallationInstallation of new water well pumps.
Pump ServiceService of existing water well pumps.
Disinfection Introduction and circulation of a carefully determined amount of an approved disinfection solution to a water well for treatment of bacteria. Additional information is available from your local Health Department: Local Health Department
HydrofracturingThe process where fluid is pumped down a well at high pressures to open fractures in adjacent rock to improve water flow to the well.
Cleaning/RedevelopmentPhysical or chemical process intended to removed sediment, scale, or other material that may be interfering with the amount of water available for pumping.
